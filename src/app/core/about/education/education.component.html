In 2010 with no previous experience in programming, I started my education at Algonquin College with the Game Development Program.

Over three years I received a Ontario College Advanced Diploma for demonstrating skills relating to game programming, art and design principles.

I learned how to generate 2D graphics with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator for various purposes such as game menus, HUDs or general graphics. I learned 3D modelling through the Autodesk 3ds Max platform to create characters, environments and other various game models. I was also introduced to game design theory and principles through history and philopshopical classes tailored to these aspects.

While this program taught me all aspects - I throughly enjoyed, excelled and chose to specialize in programming of all kinds.

I learned many programming languages such as C++, C#, Objective-C, Flash/Actionscript, Rudimentary C, HTML/CSS and Java. I was also introduced to various programming engines and frameworks such as Unity, Unreal and Microsoft XNA. I used tools such as Visual Studio, Xcode, Adobe Dreamweaver and Visual Studio Code. I also was given the opportunity to learn graphics programming through the HLSL and GLSL languages as well as using the OpenGL framework.
